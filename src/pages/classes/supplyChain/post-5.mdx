# Definitions

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;

**Procurement:** the process of selecting and vetting suppliers. It includes purchasing maangement, strategic sourcing, supplier relationship management.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;

**Request for Proposal (RFP):** a document used to determine a supplier's capability and interest in the production fo a customized product or service.  

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;

**Purchase Requisition:** an internal document requesting a material like a monitor.  

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;

**Purhcase Order:** an external document becoming legally binding ONLY when supplier accepts it.  

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;

# The Basic of Purchasing Process (The Steps)

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;

Problem: Someone in the company needs a new monitor.   
Step: They send a purchase requisition document.     
Step: Authorization Required.  

OR  

Problem: The MRP automatically detects we need more plastic for our materials.    
Step: The MRP sends a virtual purchase requistion.    
Step: We identify possible suppliers.  
Step: Select supplier automatically OR bidding process may happen.  
Step: Create purchase order document (PO). It's a document that says "Hey, you [the other company] are going to supply my company x amount of goods for x amount of money."  
Step: Process of waiting for order.
Step 8: Receive the order.
Step 9: Receive Invoice.
Step 10: Do a Reconciliation (3-way match). When company has an invoice, and you check if the price and quantity in the **invoice** and **purchase order** match and that you have that you actually **recevied the goods**.
Step 11: Payment.
Step 12: Check taxes are good.
Step 13: Close the purchase order - take it out of the system.
Step 14: Analysis. The purchasing department analyzes.
