# Add or Drop Decisions

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;

For these types of questions we are always talking about adding or dropping stores, segments or departments.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;

We drop a store if we loose more money when keeping the store.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;

We keep the store if we loose more money when dropping the store.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;

# Make or Buy Decisions

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;

To decide if we will make or buy something- we focus on the cost of doing so. Whichever decision requires less money is the one we choose.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;

If it costs less money to buy then we buy it.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;

If it costs less money to make then we make it.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;

# Accept or Reject Special Order

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;

The Sharp Pencil company manufactures packs of pencils and sells them for $5 a pack. Variable manufacturing costs is $2 a pack, and allocated fixed manufacturing cost is $1 a pair. It has enough idle capacity avaliable to accept a one time only special order of 15,000 packs of pencils at $3 a pack. Sharp Pencil company will not incur any marketing results as a result of the special order. What would the effect on operating income be if the special order could be accepted without affecting normal sales:

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;

<li style="list-style:none">A) $16,000 increase </li>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;

<li style="list-style:none">B) $16,000 decrease   </li>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;

<li style="list-style:none">C) $15,000 increase  </li>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;

<li style="list-style:none">D) $15,000 decrease </li>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;

<details>
  <summary>Solution</summary>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;

  **Answer: C**

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;

</details>
