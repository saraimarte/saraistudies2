# FAQ

<FAQ question = "How do I know when to use trig sub?">

We use trig sub on specific types of problems, most of the time the problems have a 'tell', it's what *tells* you that 1. you can use trig sub and 2. which triangle you're going to use. 

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;

The 'tell' or the 'main thing' as I call it sometimes is a **root that contains an x<sup>2</sup> and an a<sup>2</sup>.**   

</FAQ>

<FAQ question = "How do I memorize the triangles? (You don't)">

</FAQ>

<FAQ question = "How do I know which triangle to use?">

You pick the triangle that contains the form of your 'tell' or the 'main thing'. See the first FAQ.

</FAQ>

<FAQ question = "What other things do I need to be aware of to solve these problems?">
**1.** Make sure your final answer does *not* contain a θ.   
**2.** Recall that you can rewrite some trig functions in terms of sin and cos (See Practice #2 <a href = "https://youtu.be/DskUROMkPrg?t=442">7:22</a>  )  
**3.** Know the unit circle.  
**4.** Know your trig identities (See previous lesson).   
**5.** Recall that if you have sin, the inverse of sin is csc but it can also be written as 1/sin.    
**6.** How to complete the square.  
**7.** Recall that just because you *can* doesn't mean you *should*. Sometimes you *can* use trig sub but you *should* use u-sub because it's easier and it...works.


</FAQ>
